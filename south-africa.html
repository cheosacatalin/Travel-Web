Taj Cape Town

Cape Town, South Africa

Location

If you like urban locales, The Taj is perfect: part of the city’s oldest precinct, opposite St George’s Cathedral, Slave Lodge and the Company Gardens, where the city’s roots were literally planted (and now home to the South African Museum, National Art Gallery, Planetarium, Holocaust Museum).

Be sure to take the complimentary city walking tour (departs Tuesday to Saturday at 10.30am). Led by the hotel concierge, you’re orientated not only to city sites but the many restaurants, coffee shops, bars and shopping options strolling distance away. By the end of the two-and-a-half hours the concierge will be ready to make any bookings on your behalf, including tours beyond the city centre.

Style & Character

The historic aspect is wonderful, with a restrained, stern street façade embellished with bronze symbolising the financial strength, stability and wealth of the Reserve Bank. The interiors are more impressive, with the vaulting banking hall converted into the hotel lounge, imbued with an old-money-beats-nouveau-riche atmosphere.

The original Heritage Wing rooms are decorated in traditionally luxurious style – the kind of soothing five-star room found the world over; Heritage Suites are characterised by high ceilings and Neoclassical detailing. Those in the modern extension, called The Tower, are relatively bland architecturally and the rooms slightly smaller, but décor is equally plush.

Service & facilities

The Taj sets the service bar high. It’s a great fusion of warm African hospitality and the respectful “God as Guest” Indian hospitality mantra, and the authenticity of care is thoroughly disarming. Staff burnish the five-star facilities: world-class therapists in the Jiva spa, great mixologists in the cocktail bar, attentive staff in both restaurants.

The complimentary wine tasting is a fun way to meet other guests; the complimentary shuttle to and from the Waterfront is useful, there is even a luxury car provided for short transfers (subject to availability). The walking tour alone is a good reason to book.

Rooms

There are 176 well-appointed, luxurious rooms, with every mod-con you’d expect from a five-star hotel: leather beds with posturepedic mattress dressed in quality linen and rich brocades; thick-pile carpets; heavy drapes and double-glazed windows. Every room has a well-appointed desk catering for the business traveller. The marble bathrooms feature walk in showers and separate tubs; those in the Tower have windows into the bedroom so they share the view (a choice between the Mountain and the City).

The Heritage rooms (floors 2-6) are more atmospheric, and don’t cost more than Tower rooms; the latter have the more panoramic views but lower ceilings and bland aluminium window/door fittings. Mountain views, regardless of location, are a bit more expensive.

Food & Drink

There are three on-site dining options: Bombay Brasserie is generally considered the most authentic fine-dining Indian restaurant in the city (don’t miss the tandoori cauliflower, and the kali mirch ka murga, a creamy chicken tikka with mint and roughly ground pepper). The street-facing Twankey Bar, housed in the old Temple Chambers opposite Parliament, is an informal meeting place that specialises in oysters and a simple gastro-pub menu washed down with champagne or a great selection of cocktails.

Mint is a casual bistro that opens entirely to pedestrian St Georges Mall and serves the kind of all-day menu you find all over the world but with additional Indian choices like muttar paneer. You are also within walking distance of a plethora of city dining options.

Price

Double Superior rooms from ZAR 1,995 (£114) in low season; from ZAR 4,575 (£260) in high season. Breakfast included. Free basic Wi-Fi for up to four devices.

Guests with disabilities

An electronic ramp for wheelchairs to gain access to foyer and main public areas, and two rooms that are slightly larger, with showers big enough to accommodate a wheelchair, and equipped with a railing and chair.
