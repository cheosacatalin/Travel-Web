Burj Al Arab Jumeirah

Dubai, United Arab Emirates

Location

Burj Al Arab is set on its own man-made island 280m (918ft) offshore and connected to the mainland by a gently curving causeway. The hotel is nine miles (15km) south of Downtown and 15.5 miles (25km) from Dubai International Airport; Wild Wadi Waterpark is located out front and Madinat Jumeirah, a dining, entertainment and hotel complex, is next door.

Style & Character

Designed in the shape of a billowing sail – a nod to the nation’s seafaring heritage – the landmark building is stunning in its simplicity. The hotel’s interiors, meanwhile, scream opulence with a soaring atrium flanked by golden columns, vibrant colours inspired by the elements, lashings of gold leaf and more than 30 different types of marble (among them rare Statuario marble, which was used by Michelangelo in his masterpieces).

Service & facilities

While the interior is irrefutably OTT, the outdoor terrace is pared-back luxury at its best with sleek lines and a California-cool vibe. Seamlessly stretching more than 300ft out into the sea, it features two pools – including a saltwater one that seems to melt into the horizon – flanked by 32 butler-serviced cabanas and 120 luxurious day sunbeds. There’s a swim-up bar, sandy beach area and laidback-luxe restaurant and bar.

Talise Spa is another highlight; spread over two levels with Moroccan-style mosaics, it features light-filled treatment rooms and separate infinity pools for men and women overlooking the ocean, along with saunas, steam rooms and plunge pools.

A staff-to-suite ratio of 8:1 and a team of round-the-clock butlers mean service is second to none, although this is clearly not the place for those who don’t like being fussed over.

Rooms

The hotel has 202 suites, each one arranged over two floors with a sweeping staircase, floor-to-ceiling windows and private butler. The entry-level one-bedroom suite is decorated in shades of royal blue, deep red and bright yellow, with oversized sofas, gilt furniture and, somewhat oddly, a giant mirror on the ceiling over the bed. There’s a large desk area, complete with iMac and printer, and even the TV and espresso machine are gold. While not the most calming environment, it’s incredibly lavish. The bathroom is just as spectacular, with a whirlpool bath that could easily fit four people, bath mats so plush your feet leave a deep imprint and full-size Hermès amenities.

Food & Drink

There are nine restaurants and bars to choose from. Cocktails at Gold on 27 are a must; the city views at night are mesmerising and the concoctions, using ingredients such as foie gras and blackened truffle oil, truly innovative. Nathan Outlaw at Al Mahara, under the stewardship of the Michelin-starred British chef and with a tropical aquarium as its centrepiece, is also heartily recommended for its simple yet very accomplished seafood. The setting is equally glamorous at Al Muntaha on the 27th floor, yet the approach is rather different; the gourmet French fare is very good but eye-wateringly expensive. On the new pool terrace, Scape is a winner, thanks to its fresh and summery ‘Californian-inspired’ cuisine with dishes like scallop tiradito and octopus salad. Breakfast is served in one of three restaurants; the informal Bab Al Yam overlooking the pool deck has a good spread with all the usual hot and cold dishes accounted for.

Price

Double rooms from AED 4,490 (£1,000) in low season; and from AED 12,000 (£2,674) in high season. Breakfast included. Free Wi-Fi.

Guests with disabilities

One dedicated suite with elevator, adapted bathroom and two alarm systems close to the bed.
