Margutta 19

Rome, Italy

Location

The cobbled backstreet lane of Via Margutta, between the Spanish Steps and Piazza del Popolo, has long been Rome’s most celebrated artists’ street. Although gentrification long ago forced out studios that were rented by the likes of Picasso, it still maintains an air of cultured chic. Many of the houses on the eastern side of the street, such as Margutta 19, have secret gardens that climb the hill towards Villa Medici.

Style & Character

This is the most luxurious of the four Rome Luxury Suites properties (the other three are all nearby), and the only one with full hotel services – a cachet that is reflected in its higher price tag. But it still feels like a well-kept secret; a private members club as much as a hotel.

Service & facilities

The entrance desk offers 24-hour concierge service, with turndown and housekeeping discreet and efficient. Staff members are gracious and multilingual, and while there is no spa or fitness area, they can recommend nearby day spas, and the garden terrace area of the restaurant is available for guests’ use outside of meal times. One nice extra is owner Alberto Moncada’s ‘Roman Notebook’ of recommended places to eat, drink, shop and visit in the area, which is given free to guests.

Rooms

With their palette of gold, chocolate, sand and parchment, the 16 spacious rooms and suites combine suave contemporary elegance with a pleasingly retro nod to Italian interiors of the Forties and Fifties – also hinted at in the magnificent, spacious bathrooms lined in travertine marble. The standout is the Garden Suite, a separate little one-bedroom house with its own private patio terrace.

The other rooms and suites either face the garden and restaurant terrace or quiet Via Margutta. If pushed, opt for one of the former – but there’s little in it in terms of ambient noise levels, which are low in both cases. There are Nespresso machines in each room, and drinks in the minibar are complimentary.

Food & Drink

Breakfast – a small but well curated buffet – is served downstairs in the chic Assaggia bar and restaurant, which extends onto a small terrace when weather permits. At lunch and dinner the restaurant, which is helmed by Michelin-starred chef Angelo Troiani, offers an innovative ‘Roman tapas’ menu. A well-priced 24-hour room service menu takes in classics such as Caesar salad with grilled chicken.

Price

Double rooms from €350 (£310) in low season; and from €500 (£440) in high. Breakfast €20 (£18) per person. Free Wi-Fi.
