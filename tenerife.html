Bahía Del Duque

Tenerife, Spain

Location

Like a habitual Olympic gold medal winner, the Bahia del Duque owns the podium in south Tenerife when it comes to location. Offering direct access to the Playa del Duque beach promenade via tiered, lawned gardens, this mini-village in the upscale area of Costa Adeje lies a flip-flop stroll from swanky shopping. Golf is very close at hand (two courses within a 10-minute drive), and airport transfers require little more than a 15-minute dash along the motorway.

Style & Character

Modelled like a 19th-century Spanish colonial village, each pastel-pink and honey-toned building on the resort is said to represent an architectural landmark within Spanish territory. This nod to national pride is reflected in the room décor and several of the restaurant menus. From the first greeting by traditionally costumed bell boys, there's no hiding from the fact that you're in for a Canarian experience; gazing around the domed Albert Hall-size atrium at check-in lets you know it's going to be an impressive one at that.

Service & facilities

For the active-minded there are tennis courts, five outdoor pools, a 24-hour gym, and organised activities aplenty, while junior guests have a kids' club, splash areas, and plenty of sandy stuff at the doorstep. Not all of the amenities involve expending energy though.

There's also a full-service spa, a star-gazing observatory, and scenic paths through beautiful mature gardens. As the island's first, truly luxurious resort hotel, the Bahia del Duque set the bar for top-notch service; during our visit, it was good to see that the standards have been kept. All of the staff we encountered made eye contact and offered a smiley greeting.

Rooms

Though not huge, the 346 rooms and 58 suites provide classic elegance, insanely comfortable beds, and balconies or terraces, most with ocean views. On the downside, the bathrooms are disappointingly plain. Suites in the upgraded Casas Ducales quarter are more airy and come with VIP perks such as an exclusive breakfast terrace, and free evening canapés and aperitifs. Stepping up another notch, 40 colonial-style villas have private pools and 24-hour butler service.

Food & Drink

Food boredom is kept at bay with no less than 21 bars and restaurants to choose from. Everything from fine French, to Nikkei cuisine and homemade burgers is available, the quality overseen by double-Michelin-starred chefs. The breakfast buffet, taken on a garden terrace, is just one of many culinary highs we encountered. Another was at the showcase Las Aguas restaurant. Here, the evening tasting menu provided a succession of creative Canarian morsels each presented as ceramic-framed artwork and paired with the very best of local wines.

Price

Double rooms from €280 (£245) in low season; and from €410 (£360) in high. Breakfast included. Free Wi-Fi. Add an extra €56 (£50) for ocean views, or €256 (£225) for one-bedroom suites.

Guests with disabilities

There are two adapted rooms but although there is ramp access to most areas, the tiered architecture, and gardens lined with rustic pathways mean this may not be suitable for guests with disabilities.
