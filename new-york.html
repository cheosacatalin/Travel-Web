AKA Wall Street

New York, United States

Location

AKA Wall Street is in the heart of the FiDi, an area that is shedding its old fuddy-duddy banking image and fast filling up with techy and creative types and fashion magazines like Vogue. The hotel is on a quiet street near Wall Street. Nearby is the One World Trade Center, the 9/11 Memorial and Museum, the porcupine-like Oculus (a cathedral-like transit hub with an ultra posh mall), Battery Park and South Street Seaport which has shed its touristy image and now boasts unique boutiques, wine bars, and the hip Smorgasburg food market. At Pier 11 Wall St, you can take the ferry to Brooklyn or Hoboken.

Style & Character

If you want to know what it feels like to live in a fully-furnished modern apartment (studio, one bed or two bed) in one of the newly cool and central areas of the city, this is where you should stay. The warm minimal interiors of the 132 'suite' AKA Wall Street are by local architect Edward Asfour of Asfour Guzy. A spacious 20th-floor rooftop lounge and large outdoor terrace look out over the neighboring Federal Reserve Bank. The hotel has a serene, sanctuary-like feel. This is a discreet address in a fully-remodelled 1907 building.

Service & facilities

One of the hotel's most unique features is its Live It Programme, where guests can enjoy a dance class at a top local studio, have a photo lesson with a photographer or train with Amie Hoff one of the best trainers in New York (she cured my decade-long sciatica in 20 minutes by teaching me four or five simple exercises). The staff will fill your fridge with your custom food list pre arrival (gluten free or vegetarian, no problem). There is a second-floor laundry for guest use. The knowledgeable 24/7 front desk can help book restaurants, shows, taxis, valet clothes or help you get into fashion shows or movie premiers. The Technogym Fitness Center, the conference room and business centre are all free to guests. Parking is $35 (£29) a day at a nearby garage.

Rooms

The 132 suites are the size of New York apartments. The majority of the suites are generously scaled studios, but they range up in size to two bedrooms with huge outdoor terraces for lounging and eating outside. Every suite has a stylish separate kitchen well stocked with appliances (including a dishwasher), lots of crockery and cooking utensils and a coffee maker and kettle. Every room has a separate lounge area, a desk, a lavish white-tiled bathroom with Bulgari toiletries. The beds have luxury pillow-top mattresses and Sferra linens. The feel is contemporary Scandinavian-minimalism with lots of natural materials; oak, wool, marble and glass.

Food & Drink

Blue Ribbon Federal Grill is the latest outpost of the Bromberg Brothers beloved ’something for everyone’ SoHo restaurant Blue Ribbon. Guests can enjoy a thoroughly modern breakfast with dishes such as avocado toast ($11/£9), smoked scramble (eggs, lox, trout roe and rye croutons) ($19/£15) and hipster beverages like coconut water ($5/£4) and kombucha tea ($7/£6). Brunch is available 10am-4pm on weekends.

The nightly dinner menu is an eclectic mix of casual and fine dining: lamb T-bone with smoky eggplant ($39/£31) and a small plate of spicy meat balls ($15/£12) or baby kale Caesar salad ($15/£12). No room service. The front desk can arrange food delivery from one of the surrounding restaurants, or source a gourmet chef to prepare your meals in your own kitchen. If you want to go out, downtown Eataly or Fowler and Wells at the Beekman Hotel are both nearby.

Price

Studio premiums from $295 (£242) in low season; and from $425 (£349) in high. Rates are reduced by 20 per cent if you stay a week and 30 per cent if you book for a month. Free Wi-Fi.

Guests with disabilities

Seven suites are adapted for wheelchairs.
