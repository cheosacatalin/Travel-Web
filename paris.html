Le Meurice

Paris, France

Location

The hotel is located on a main road, but then that road is the rue de Rivoli, one of the most famous thoroughfares in Paris, with the Tuileries Gardens just across the way. Paris locations don’t get much more central than this: the Louvre is a 10-minute stroll through the park and the luxury boutiques of Place Vendôme and Rue Saint-Honoré are on the doorstep. There are easy links to the Marais and Champs-Élysées via line 1 (Concorde, Tuileries).

Style & Character

The porter-flanked revolving door at the grand Rivoli entrance is a portal between the buzz of ultra-central Paris and a refined space of sparkling luxury and understated discretion. The ornate grandeur (elegant floor-to-ceiling windows and glass panels, original cornicing, Doric columns) of the 19th-century building blends with exquisite 18th-century-style decorative features such as wall mouldings, hung portraits and chandeliers.

Following renovations by Philippe Starck, the most recent in 2016, the jewellery-box like splendour is punctuated with modern pieces, such as the surrealist intertwined columns in the lobby, and vinyl lounge chairs decorated with 18th-century-style cameos.

Service & facilities

Guests at the hotel have the feeling of being quietly, discreetly looked after, from the ‘Bonjour’ of the door men and women, to the low-key, efficient check-in. The concierge service can arrange just about anything. Dali reportedly paid staff to catch individual flies from the Tuileries Gardens – it feels unlikely that similarly outlandish requests today would be refused.

The Spa Valmont has its own mezzanine level and is arranged around a quiet interior courtyard. Here, guests can choose from a range of high-end body treatments and facials (charged at a supplement). A steam room, sauna and relaxation room are available for all guests, as well as a small gym.

Rooms

The hotel’s 160 rooms and suites are decorated in Versailles fashion, with Louis XVI-style armchairs and grand curtains in silk, ornate wall mouldings and chandeliers. But the spaces are comfortable, too, with plush patterned carpets and vast, welcoming beds. Tech-wise, there are wall-mounted televisions with international channels and internet access and USB ports on desks. Views are of either rue de Castiglione, a quiet interior courtyard or over the Tuileries.

Bathrooms are a decadent affair, decked out in Italian Arabesscato marble. Most feature deep bathtubs and all are equipped with Italian-style showers, plus smart robes and slippers.

The hotel’s suites take opulence to a fantasy-land level. The vast Presidential Suites on the first floor are straight out of Versailles with Hungarian parquet and immense grand salons. The jewel in the crown is the Belle Etoile suite on the seventh floor, which has its own private elevator and terrace with 360-degree view of Paris – you may recognise the view from Instagram (this is a favourite with A-listers).

Food & Drink

Executive Chef Jocelyn Herlan, formerly of The Dorchester, oversees the two Alain Ducasse restaurants. Le Meurice Alain Ducasse is the two Michelin-starred gastronomic offering, with a menu that revolves around simple, quality ingredients.

French classics are served at the second restaurant, Le Dali, as well as a truly outstanding Mediterranean menu. The sharing starters are particularly memorable: the patatas bravas (€22/£19), rounds of potato filled a glossy reduction of black garlic, Espelette and Cognac, exquisitely reinvent this dish, while the minestrone (€24/£21) is perfectly executed: delicately cooked vegetables, poured over with a light, fragrant consommé. Desserts are courtesy of pastry whizzkid Cédric Grolet, several times voted best pastry chef in the world – try one of his signature reconstructed fruits.

Breakfast is served in the grand environs of Le Meurice Alain Ducasse and features continental, American and ‘healthy’ options. Croissants are perfectly crispy, eggs well-cooked and suppliers carefully chosen (Bordier butter, juices from Parisian brand Atelier Nubio).
