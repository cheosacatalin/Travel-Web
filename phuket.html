Iniala Beach House

Natai Beach, Phuket, Thailand

Location

Setting itself apart from the crowd, Iniala is located on the golden edges of Natai beach, on the mainland, a 25-minute drive north of Phuket airport. With little else in the area aside from a handful of private homes, tangled fields and lazy buffalo, it feels pleasingly off the radar – which is exactly what most of Iniala’s high-flying guests are looking for.

Style & Character

Surreal, sexy and a little bit sci-fi, Iniala looks like it might have been stolen from the set of Barbarella. The exteriors are cosmically cool – armadillo-shaped buildings, jewel-like mosaics, infinity pools shooting into the horizon – while the interiors have been put together by 10 designers at the top of their game – including Irishman Joseph Walsh, the Brazilian Campana brothers and Mark Brazier Jones, who also designed the sets for the Fifty Shades of Grey movie.

Service & facilities

Personal butlers, chauffeurs, chefs, spa therapists, fitness instructors and nannies - the service team spirit around the property granting wishes like genies.

Rooms

The 10 rooms are spread across three three-bedroom villas and a penthouse, and are closer to elaborate theatre sets than your average luxury hotel room. Every space is completely different and extraordinary in its own way; the penthouse has “floating” furniture and soft spongy floors; the Collector’s villa wows with walls clad in broken celadon ceramics and £120,000 bed (yes really, that’s not a misprint); Villa Siam has a golden, beehive-like spa chamber, while the white-on-white Villa Bianca has swinging chairs shaped like Russian Matryoshka dolls.

In contrast, Iniala Shores, a collection of four five-bedroom villas, are calm uncluttered affairs – enormous Ibiza-esque white spaces with huge turquoise infinity pools that roll straight onto the beach. Perfect for families and group get-togethers.

Food & Drink

Naturally, all this fabulousness has food to match. Flagship restaurant Esenzi is helmed by New Yorker Tim Butler, the mastermind behind the highly-acclaimed hugely-popular Bangkok restaurants Eat Me and Bunker. Seasonal tasting menus are sophisticated with a seafood focus – delicate quail eggs topped with crème fraiche and caviar, A5 Ohmi wagyu beef teamed with tender swordfish belly, Japanese shima aji tartar.

When not dining on eight-course degustation menus, guests can enjoy a wish-list of dishes – everything from just-out-the-oven breads for breakfast to rare Royal Thai recipes - whipped up for them by head chef Sandro Aguilera.
