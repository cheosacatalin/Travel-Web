Hotel Ritz Madrid

Madrid, Spain

Location

Next door to the Prado and a few minutes’ walk from the Thyssen-Bornemisza and Reina Sofia museums, this is a great choice for art lovers. The Retiro park and the tapas bars around Plaza Santa Ana are nearby too.

Style & Character

The Ritz has undergone extensive renovations but has managed to hang onto a lot of its original features, so expect classic luxury at every turn. Sumptuous carpets made down the road at the Royal Tapestry Factory ensure that every step you take is in cushioned comfort.

Service & facilities

Everything you would expect from a hotel of this standard, and then some.

Rooms

There are 167 rooms and suites, and no two are the same. Some have balconies overlooking the square or the hotel’s gardens, others have chandeliers and silk canopies over the bed, and most are furnished with antiques and decorated with original artworks. Free Wi-Fi.

Food & Drink

The Goya restaurant is one of the best in the city. For a decadent treat, book in for the legendary Sunday brunch. In summer it is served on the lovely terrace, which is open for drinks, lunch and dinner at other times. You can also get a decent cup of tea at the Ritz, which is certainly not the case everywhere in Madrid. The afternoon tea is rather special and a glass of Champagne at the Krug bar slips down very well after an afternoon in the Prado.

Price

This is obviously not a budget option, but you do feel you are getting your money’s worth.

Guests with disabilities

Yes – adapted rooms.
